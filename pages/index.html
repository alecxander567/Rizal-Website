The Life of a National Hero

🏡
✦ June 19, 1861

Birth in Calamba, Laguna

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born the seventh of eleven children to Francisco Rizal Mercado and Teodora Alonso Realonda, a family of mixed Chinese, Spanish, and Tagalog descent.

🎓
✦ 1872 — 1877

Ateneo Municipal de Manila

Rizal excelled in academics, literature, painting, sculpture, and poetry. He graduated as one of nine students awarded the title of Sobresaliente — the highest academic honor — and composed his first poem at age 8.

⛵
✦ 1882 — 1892

Journey to Europe, Asia & America

Rizal secretly left the Philippines at 21, traveling through Spain, France, Germany, England, Belgium, Austria, Switzerland, Japan, and the United States — earning two degrees, writing two novels, and awakening a nation.

📖
✦ 1887

Noli Me Tangere Published

Rizal published his first novel in Berlin, Germany. The novel exposed the abuses of the Spanish colonial government and the Catholic clergy, igniting the fire of nationalism across the Philippine archipelago.

⚔️
✦ 1891

El Filibusterismo Published

The darker sequel, published in Ghent, Belgium. More politically charged than Noli, the novel depicted revolution as the only answer to colonial oppression — dedicated to the martyred priests Gomez, Burgos, and Zamora.

🕯️
✦ December 30, 1896

Martyrdom at Bagumbayan

At 35, Rizal was executed by firing squad at Bagumbayan Field, now Luneta Park. He faced death calmly, turning to face the rising sun. His sacrifice ignited the Philippine Revolution and cemented his legacy as the national hero.

Literary & Scientific Legacy

His Immortal Works

📖
Novel

Noli Me Tangere

A scathing exposé of Spanish colonial rule. "Touch Me Not" — the untouched social cancers festering in Philippine society. Banned by the Church. Beloved by every Filipino.

✦ Berlin, 1887
⚔️
Novel

El Filibusterismo

The darker sequel — revolution as the inevitable fruit of oppression. Dedicated to the martyred priests Gomez, Burgos, and Zamora. Published in Ghent, 1891.

✦ Ghent, 1891
✒️
Poetry

Mi Último Adiós

His farewell poem, written on the eve of execution and hidden inside an alcohol lamp. One of the most moving poems ever written in any language.

✦ Fort Santiago, 1896
🏛️
Essay

La Solidaridad Articles

Political essays advocating Philippine representation in the Spanish Cortes and equal rights for Filipinos. Published in Madrid's reform newspaper.

✦ Madrid, 1889–1895
🔬
Science

Ophthalmological Studies

A trained ophthalmologist who treated the poor for free. He famously operated on his own mother's cataracts in Hong Kong, restoring her sight.

✦ Paris, Heidelberg & Hong Kong
🎨
Arts & Language

Polymath & Sculptor

Rizal spoke 22 languages, painted, sculpted, wrote poetry, and conducted zoological research in Dapitan. He was the most brilliant Filipino of his age.
