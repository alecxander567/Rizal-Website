1869 – 1871
Age 3 – 5 · Calamba, Laguna
Home Education
Calamba, Laguna, Philippines

Before any classroom, there was his mother. Teodora Alonso taught young José the alphabet, prayers, and his first poems before he was five years old. She read to him nightly and recited verses, planting in him a love of language that no university could have given.

Reading Writing Prayer Poetry
✦ Learned to read and write by age 5 under the sole instruction of his mother.

1871 – 1872
Age 9 – 10 · Biñan, Laguna
Biñan Latin School
Biñan, Laguna, Philippines

Sent to Biñan to study under Justiniano Aquino Cruz, a teacher known for strict discipline. The young Rizal arrived knowing more than his classmates and quickly surpassed them all. Within days he outperformed the top student. Cruz's rigorous methods in Latin and Spanish formed the linguistic backbone of everything Rizal would write.

Latin Spanish Arithmetic Religious Doctrine
✦ Surpassed all classmates within days — the top student was defeated in his first recitation contest.

1872 – 1877
Age 11 – 16 · Manila
Ateneo Municipal de Manila
Intramuros, Manila, Philippines

The Jesuit-run Ateneo was the crucible of Rizal's genius. Under the guidance of Fr. Francisco de Paula Sánchez, he developed his poetry, rhetoric, and classical education. He earned the highest marks in nearly every subject and graduated with a Bachelor of Arts with the grade of Sobresaliente (Excellent) — the top of his class. He also began sculpting and painting here.

Rhetoric Philosophy Latin Greek Natural History Poetry
✦ Graduated Sobresaliente — won the gold medal for excellence. At 18, won the literary contest with A La Juventud Filipina.

1877 – 1882
Age 16 – 21 · Manila
University of Santo Tomas
Sampaloc, Manila, Philippines

Rizal enrolled in UST to study philosophy and letters, then shifted to medicine — primarily to treat his mother's failing eyesight. He excelled academically but chafed under the Dominican friars' biased treatment of Filipino students. Frustrated by the discrimination, and with Paciano's support, he resolved to continue his studies in Spain where he could breathe and learn freely.

Medicine Philosophy Letters Natural Sciences
✦ Left UST without completing his degree, determined to finish medical studies in Madrid where he would be treated as an equal.

1882 – 1885
Age 21 – 24 · Madrid, Spain
Universidad Central de Madrid
Madrid, Spain

In Madrid, Rizal flourished. He completed his Licentiate in Medicine with honors while simultaneously pursuing a second degree in Philosophy and Letters. He also joined the Círculo Hispano-Filipino, wrote prolifically for the reform press, and began writing Noli Me Tángere in the evenings after his studies were done.

Medicine Surgery Philosophy Letters Fine Arts
✦ Earned his Licentiate in Medicine and Licentiate in Philosophy & Letters — simultaneously — with honors.

1885 – 1886
Age 24 – 25 · Paris, France
University of Paris
Paris, France

After Madrid, Rizal moved to Paris to specialize in ophthalmology under Dr. Louis de Wecker, one of the foremost eye surgeons in Europe. Paris also exposed him to the vibrant intellectual and artistic circles of the continent. He immersed himself in French culture, improved his French, and continued work on his novel while training in surgery.

Ophthalmology Eye Surgery Clinical Medicine French Language
✦ Trained under Dr. Louis de Wecker — Europe's leading ophthalmologist — mastering the surgical technique he would later use on his own mother.

1886 – 1887
Age 25 – 26 · Heidelberg, Germany
University of Heidelberg
Heidelberg, Germany

Rizal's time in Heidelberg was among his most productive. He trained under Dr. Otto Becker at the University Eye Clinic, mastering advanced ophthalmological techniques. He learned German, explored the German academic tradition, and in Berlin — just after leaving Heidelberg — he finally published Noli Me Tángere in February 1887.

Ophthalmology Clinical Research German Language Natural Science
✦ Under Dr. Otto Becker, Rizal became a fully qualified ophthalmologist. Noli Me Tángere was published in Berlin while he was here.
Academic Credentials

Degrees & Distinctions

📜 Bachelor of Arts
Bachillerato en Artes
Ateneo Municipal de Manila

Graduated with the highest grade of Sobresaliente (Excellent) in 1877, topping his class. The Jesuits at Ateneo recognized him as one of the most gifted students they had ever taught. He also earned a gold medal for academic excellence.

1877 · Manila, Philippines
⚕️ Licentiate in Medicine
Licenciado en Medicina
Universidad Central de Madrid

Completed his medical degree in 1884 with honors, specializing in ophthalmology. He later completed his doctorate in absentia but never formally received it due to his political situation in Spain. He practiced medicine throughout his life, treating patients in Dapitan for free.

1884 · Madrid, Spain
📚 Licentiate in Philosophy
Licenciado en Filosofía y Letras
Universidad Central de Madrid

Simultaneously with his medical degree, Rizal earned his Licentiate in Philosophy and Letters — a remarkable feat of intellectual stamina. This second degree reflected his insatiable hunger for knowledge across disciplines, from science to literature to history.

1885 · Madrid, Spain
👁️ Ophthalmology Specialization
Advanced Ophthalmological Training
Paris & Heidelberg

Trained under two of Europe's greatest eye surgeons — Dr. Louis de Wecker in Paris and Dr. Otto Becker in Heidelberg. He became an expert in cataract surgery and performed the procedure on his own mother in 1887 with successful results.

1885–1887 · Paris & Heidelberg
🏆 Literary Prize
A La Juventud Filipina
Liceo Artistico-Literario de Manila

At just 18 years of age, Rizal won first prize in a literary contest with his poem A La Juventud Filipina (To the Filipino Youth). It was the first time a native Filipino was awarded this honor, beating out Spanish and mestizo competitors.

1879 · Manila, Philippines
🔬 Scientific Contribution
New Species Discoveries
Dapitan Exile, Mindanao

During his exile in Dapitan, Rizal engaged in ethnological and biological research. He discovered and documented several new species of animals, which were named after him — including Draco rizali (a lizard) and Rachophorus rizali (a frog) — cementing his place in science.

1892–1896 · Dapitan, Philippines
